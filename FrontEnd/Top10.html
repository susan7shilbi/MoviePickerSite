The Godfather (1972) 9.2


Francis Ford Coppola and Mario Puzo co-wrote the script for The Godfather, an American crime movie that was released in 1972. It is based on Puzo's best-selling 1969 novel of the same name. Marlon Brando, Al Pacino, James Caan, Richard Castellano, Robert Duvall, Sterling Hayden, John Marley, Richard Conte, and Diane Keaton are among the actors that appear in the movie. It is the first film in the trilogy about The Godfather.

The Dark Knight (2008) 9.0


A superhero movie called The Dark Knight was released in 2008, and Christopher Nolan co-wrote the screenplay with his brother Jonathan. It is the sequel to Batman Begins (2005) and the second film in The Dark Knight Trilogy, all of which are based on the DC Comics superhero Batman. As they team together to fight organised crime in Gotham City, the vigilante Batman, police lieutenant James Gordon, and district attorney Harvey Dent are all depicted in the movie.

Pulp Fiction (1994) 8.9


The 1994 American black comedy crime film Pulp Fiction was created by Quentin Tarantino and Roger Avary. It was written and directed by Tarantino. It explores numerous tales of crime in Los Angeles and features John Travolta, Samuel L. Jackson, Bruce Willis, Tim Roth, Ving Rhames, and Uma Thurman. The title alludes to the pulp publications and hardboiled crime novels that were popular in the middle of the 20th century and were distinguished by their vivid violence and snappy wit.

Jai Bhim (2021) 8.4


Victory to Bhim is the title of the 2021 period legal drama film Jai Bhim, which was written and directed by T. J. Gnanavel and produced by Jyothika and Suriya under the banner of 2D Entertainment. Suriya, Lijomol Jose, and Manikandan are the main actors in the movie. Rao Ramesh, Prakash Raj, Rajisha Vijayan, and others play supporting parts. The movie examines state violence and police racism against a vulnerable group.

3 Idiots (2009) 8.4


The coming-of-age comedy-drama 3 Idiots, which was released in 2009 in the Hindi language, was written, edited, and directed by Rajkumar Hirani. Producer Vidhu Vinod Chopra also contributed to the screenplay. The movie, which is partially based on Chetan Bhagat's novel Five Point Someone, stars Omi Vaidya, Kareena Kapoor, and Boman Irani in crucial parts and Aamir Khan, R. Madhavan, and Sharman Joshi in the title roles, marking their return three years after the 2006 movie Rang De Basanti.

Pariyerum Perumal (2018) 8.3


Pariyerum Perumal, which translates to "The God who mounts a Horse," is a 2018 Tamil-language drama film produced by Pa. Ranjith and directed by Mari Selvaraj on his directorial debut. In addition to Yogi Babu and G. Marimuthu, who play supporting roles, the movie stars Kathir and Anandhi in the key roles. Santhosh Narayanan wrote the soundtrack for the movie. It was awarded as the best movies in the year 2018.

Dangal (2016) 8.3


Dangal, which translates to "Wrestling Competition," is a 2016 Hindi-language biographical sports drama film that was directed by Nitesh Tiwari and produced by Aamir Khan, Kiran Rao, and The Walt Disney Company India's Siddharth Roy Kapur. Khan plays Mahavir Singh Phogat in the movie, a pehlwani amateur wrestler who develops Geeta Phogat and Babita Kumari into India's first elite female wrestlers.

Jersey (2019) 8.3


In 2019, Gowtam Tinnanuri released Jersey, a sports drama movie in the Telugu language. Suryadevara Naga Vamsi, under the banner of Sithara Entertainments, produced the movie. Ronit Kamra, Sathyaraj, Harish Kalyan, Sanusha, Sampath Raj, and Viswant Duddumpudi play crucial roles in addition to Nani and Shraddha Srinath, who play the lead characters. Sanu John Varghese handled the film's cinematography, while Anirudh Ravichander created the soundtrack. Naveen Nooli handled the editing.
